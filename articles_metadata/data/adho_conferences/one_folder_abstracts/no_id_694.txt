The importance of scientific visualization for basic and applied research has been recognized as an importance aspect of scientific practise in many disciplines. Recent research trends in the Humanities in general and in Digital Humanities in particular are no exception in this respect. The goal of the present paper is threefold: (i) to survey different types of scientific visualizations needed for language data, (ii) to describe a set of web applications that have been implemented in the context of the CLARIN-D project , and (iii) to demonstrate the added value of visualization.
